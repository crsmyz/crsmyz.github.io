Glassbox App

Glassbox is a national legislation tracking app that gives users information on the status of legislation moving through the government and details about what the legislation contains. It allows users to filter through the legislation they see by choosing interests that are relevant to them and gives them access to social media tools that allow users to share with friends and give feedback on legislation to lawmakers.

Responsibilities

  1. Information architecture
  2. Personas
  3. Product map
  4. User flows
  5. Page layout
  6. Icon design
  7. Logo design

Goals

  • Enable users to easily track legislation stats, history and position inside of the government.
  • Help users filter, navigate, read and understand all forms of legislation moving through government.
  • Give users tools for easy and effective communication with lawmakers on legislation and issues.

Challenges

  • User attention span may not be maintained by any given piece of legislation due to the length of content, surrounding environment and the competition for attention from other apps.
  • App may be used predominantly by the more politically inclined and informed which limits effectiveness with a broader audience.
  • Large pieces of legislation are unwieldy and difficult to read on a small mobile device.
  • Legislation contains legal jargon, difficult language and structure that can be hard to understand.
  • Difficult to find information of value to address with lawmakers or share with friends.

Solutions

  • Use legislation summaries, and status tracking that gives information such as location of legislation, votes, filibustering, passing, failing and signing.
  • Subdivide legislation into short digestible sections so users can quickly navigate and comprehend a large amount of content.
  • Use embedded legal jargon definitions and translations to enable easy understanding while reading legislation.
  • Allow for users to provide personal and professional interests that relate to the legislation they want to see.
